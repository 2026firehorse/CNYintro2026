The Spring Festival

More Than Just
A Single Day

Chinese New Year (CNY), historically known as the Spring Festival (春节), is a grand 15-day celebration marking the end of winter and the fresh beginnings of spring.

It acts as a massive "reset button" for your life. The core purpose is to symbolically clear out the old year’s clutter and misfortune (literally and metaphorically) to make way for new prosperity.

The World's Largest Migration

Core Values

Why It Matters: Family & Identity

At its heart, Chinese New Year is deeply connected to family and identity. Family reunion is the absolute core value of the festival.

  • flight_takeoff People travel thousands of miles to return home (Chunyun), reinforcing bonds and cultural continuity.
  • favorite Regardless of age, success, or location, returning home for the New Year is considered an emotionally non-negotiable pilgrimage.

Northern Tradition

In northern China, dumplings (Jiaozi) are the star of the show. They resemble ancient gold ingots, so eating them is symbolic of "taking in wealth" for the year ahead.

The Spring Festival Gala (Chunwan)

Known as "Chunwan," this televised variety show is a modern tradition broadcast since the 1980s. It is one of the most-watched TV shows in the world.

Ancient Folklore

Why All The Red?

The Legend of Nian

Long ago, a terrifying beast named "Nian" (年) lived in the mountains. On the eve of every new year, it would descend upon villages to devour livestock and people.

Villagers lived in fear until they discovered Nian's three weaknesses. The beast was terrified of:

  • palette The Color Red
  • light_mode Bright Lights
  • volume_up Loud Noises

"And so, the tradition was born. Red lanterns, red scrolls, and loud firecrackers are used to this day to keep the beast—and bad luck—at bay."

The "Fu" Mystery

You see the character "Fu" upside down. Why? It's a pun! "Upside down" (倒) sounds like "arrive" (到).
